# Cuebook Market Signal Grammar

## Shared Composition

- Canvas: `720 x 420`.
- Upper left: signal/as-of time, timezone, then two to four readable strategy tags.
- Upper right: quiet Cuebook wordmark.
- Main field: one creator action headline and one information object.
- Lower field: one human reason or transmission mechanism.
- No visible source count, settlement sentence, provisional legend, footer, or decorative chart.

Whitespace is active capacity around the evidence. It cannot be used to hide missing provenance or make a weak fact appear important.

## Key Number

Use when the number itself changes the market reading.

Reading order:

1. creator action, including asset and horizon;
2. dominant tabular number;
3. number label;
4. one reason explaining why this number changes the trade.

Optional comparison text may be shown only when it is part of the same sourced measurement, such as `vs. consensus +3.2%` or `20-day percentile 94`.

## Key News

Use when one verified event can carry a creator's trade expression.

Reading order:

1. publication time and publisher;
2. readable strategy tags;
3. creator action, including asset and horizon;
4. the source headline introduced by `因为`;
5. one price mechanism explaining the trade.

Do not turn the interpretation into a multi-step causal chain. Route a developed mechanism to a logic card or argument-plus-curve figure.

## Public And Private Layers

- Public: action, reason, asset, horizon, and strategy tags.
- Private: `observed`, `derived`, `provisional`, `unconfirmed`, source refs, retrieval details, formula lineage, and evidence gaps.
- Color follows the trade expression: long, short, relative value, volatility, or hedge. It does not expose evidence status.
- Public tags describe the play, such as `事件驱动`, `预期修正`, `相对价值`, or `流动性挤压`; they never describe workflow state.

## Receptive Restraint

The profile is informed by Hara Design Institute writing on white as a sensory and communicative resource. Cuebook adapts that principle through financial hierarchy, tabular numerals, source lineage, and explicit evidence states. Avoid ornamental Japanese motifs or claims of stylistic imitation.
