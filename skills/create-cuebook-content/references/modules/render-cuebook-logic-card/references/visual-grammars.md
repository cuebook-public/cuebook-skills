# Cuebook Visual Grammars

| Grammar | Use when | Required content | Compact limit |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `causal_chain` | A news event or catalyst moves through a short mechanism | event/evidence, mechanism, market effect | 4 main nodes + 2 challenge/condition nodes |
| `metric_thesis` | A fundamental judgment depends on a small set of numbers | thesis, 2-4 metrics, invalidation/countercase | 4 metrics |
| `scenario_tree` | Several observable conditions lead to different outcomes | current setup, 2-3 scenarios | 3 branches in Feed |
| `evidence_balance` | Support and counterevidence both matter | support, challenge, explicit conclusion | 3 items per side |
| `comparison` | The decision is relative | primary, benchmark, shared dimensions | 4 dimensions |
| `price_timeline` | Price, trigger, target, invalidation, range, or settlement clock carries the view | market series and explicit levels | route to thesis-chart skill |

## Shared Semantics

- `observed`: solid neutral or source-tinted treatment;
- `derived`: solid support treatment with supporting fact refs and a neutral `inference` state label;
- `conditional`: yellow border or dashed edge;
- `unresolved`: muted red/yellow treatment;
- `challenges`: red edge toward the claim it weakens;
- `settles`: yellow footer or terminal node tied to the exact deadline.

One graphic answers one question. A hybrid Feed card may place one logic card beside one market chart, but neither should repeat the other's job.

Public graphics show the argument, evidence state, countercase, and settlement only. Internal enrichment notes, prompt history, rewrite notes, and `cuebook_contribution` never appear as visual blocks.
